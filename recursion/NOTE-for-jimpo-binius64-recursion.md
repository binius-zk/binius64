## 1. Summary / contribution

The Binius64 IOP verifier is O(circuit size): essentially all of its per-proof arithmetic is the "monster" evaluation `check_eval` performs (`crates/verifier/src/protocols/shift/verify.rs:207-319`) вҖ” on real proofs we measured a **median 51.7M GF(2^128) mults per verify** (26 proofs, range 12.5MвҖ“101M), of which **~99.93% is the monster**. You already ship the escape hatch: `compute_public_value` (`crates/ip/src/channel.rs:87-111`) hoists the monster to a single inout wire in `IronSpartanBuilderChannel`, so the O(N) work disappears from the recursion circuit and is instead recomputed *natively by whoever verifies the outer proof*. That makes the **circuit** succinct but not the **verifier**: for K aggregated leaves the final verifier still pays K native monster passes.

This note describes a protocol that closes that last gap. Fix one CS shape S. Commit the constraint-term table **once** as a per-shape verification key. Then, for a batch of K same-shape proofs, discharge all K deferred monster claims with **one** batched argument. It collapses the K native monster passes (KВ·O(N)) the final verifier would otherwise pay into a single O(N) table pass вҖ” or, in the fully-succinct variant, into a fixed FRI/opening endgame independent of both N and K вҖ” leaving in either case only a sub-dominant KВ·polylog eq/h_op residual. Everything is built from primitives you already have вҖ” `sumcheck::batch_verify`, `sumcheck::verify` over a bivariate product, `fracaddcheck::verify`, `basefold::verify` вҖ” plus your ZK-wrap and your `compute_public_value` deferral hook. There is no new PCS, no new IOP, and no new field machinery. We built it, ran it on real proofs, and had it independently re-audited; the succinct discharge (STEP 2) verifies in **4 ms** at K=3 вҖ” its dominant endgame flat in N (N=15 вҶ’ 1 ms, N=24.5M вҶ’ 4 ms, FRI log-depth only) and K-independent by construction, with only the sub-dominant KВ·polylog residual вҖ” and the integrated wrap (K leaves + discharge on one Fiat-Shamir stream) verifies K=3 real leaves in **18 ms** versus **361 ms** for native re-verification.

We note up front: you flagged `compute_public_value` as `HACK вҖҰ This feature should be killed and handled more elegantly` (`channel.rs:105-106`). Our experience is the opposite of "kill it" вҖ” it is exactly the right seam for recursion, and this note is in part an argument that the deferral hook deserves a first-class, sound discharge rather than removal. The instantiation we built and measured targets an all-BitAnd CS shape (`T_mul = вҲ…`); the general two-lane extension is mechanical and sketched in В§3.6.

## 2. Background: why the verifier is O(N), and the deferral hook

**The monster.** `check_eval` closes the shift reduction by asserting `witness_eval В· monster_eval == eval` (`shift/verify.rs:315-316`), where `monster_eval` is your batched multilinear evaluation of the constraint matrices. Re-associating `evaluate_monster_multilinear_for_operation` (`monster.rs:123-149`) and `evaluate_matrices` (`monster.rs:165-219`), for an all-BitAnd shape it is exactly

```
monster_eval(c) = ОЈ_{t < N}  О»_and^{m_t+1} В· h_{op_t} В· eq6(s_t, r_s) В· eq(x_t, r_x') В· eq(y_t, r_y)
```

- `t` indexes the `N = ОЈ_x (|a|+|b|+|c|)` `ShiftedValueIndex` occurrences (AND arity 3);
- `m_t вҲҲ {0,1,2}` is the operand slot вҶ’ the weight `О»_and^{m+1}` is your `lambda_powers = powers(О»).skip(1)` (`monster.rs:137-140`);
- `op_t вҲҲ [8]` is the `ShiftVariant` вҶ’ `h_{op}` is your `evaluate_h_op` output (`monster.rs:27`);
- `s_t` is the 6-bit shift amount вҶ’ `eq6(s_t, r_s)` is the `evaluate_inplace_scalars(В·, r_s)` fold (`monster.rs:144`);
- `x_t` is the constraint index вҶ’ `eq(x_t, r_x')` is a read into `r_x_prime_tensor` (built at `monster.rs:135`, read in the scatter at `:179`/`:197`);
- `y_t = value_index` вҶ’ `eq(y_t, r_y)` is a read into `r_y_tensor` (`monster.rs:147`) **[on current main this tensor is SEGMENTED вҖ” `R[y_t]` per the Addendum, built at `shift/verify.rs:435-445` вҖ” and the claim point gains a trailing `r_segment`; `eq(y_t, r_y)` is the pre-`#1554` form]**.

The cost is one GF(2^128) mult **per term** (the scatter `evals[shift_id][amount] += constraint_eval В· r_y_tensor[value_index]`, `monster.rs:197-198`) plus the three eq-tensor materializations `2^a, 2^m, 2^w` (`eq_ind_partial_eval_scalars`, `crates/math/src/multilinear/eq.rs:225-241`) plus `evaluate_h_op` / `l_tilde` (~7K). So `monster_eval вүҲ T_and + T_mul + 2^a + 2^m + 2^w + ~7K`. Everything else in `IOPVerifier::verify` (`crates/verifier/src/verify.rs:105-265`) вҖ” intmul prodcheck/GKR, bitand, shift sumchecks, ring-switch, BaseFold/FRI вҖ” is polylog, except the O(public) pubcheck `evaluate_inplace_scalars` (`verify.rs:242`). We modeled the residual after removing the monster as **~36K mults + ~12K SHA-256 compressions** per child; the hash count matches the measured 12.2K almost exactly, confirming the monster is the whole story.

**The hook.** The monster is wrapped in `channel.compute_public_value(&inputs, closure)` (`shift/verify.rs:254-307`). Its inputs are the 61-scalar claim point `c = [r_zhat', О»_and, О»_int, r_x'_and(23), r_x'_mul(0), r_j(6), r_s(6), r_y(23)]` (`shift/verify.rs:244-252`), all public-coin. In `IronSpartanBuilderChannel` the closure is **skipped** and the result becomes one inout wire (`builder_channel.rs:224-243`, via `combine_varlen` with an all-public input path); the contract (`channel.rs:87-111`) states this replaces "a sub-circuit's worth of constraints." Symbolic execution of the whole leaf verifier through this channel is exactly your ZK-wrap (landed #1448, present at our pin); we repurposed it as recursion.

**The gap.** Deferral removes ~99.93% of the mults *from the circuit*, but the deferred value `v = monster_eval(c)` still has to be produced and certified somewhere. In the one-level wrap that "somewhere" is the final verifier's native recompute вҖ” O(N) per leaf, paid K times for K leaves. That is the non-succinctness this note removes.

## 3. The discharge protocol

Fix a shape S with term table T = the flattened list of N tuples `П„_t = (x_t, y_t, s_t, op_t, m_t)`, one per `ShiftedValueIndex` occurrence (source: `crates/core/src/constraint_system.rs:134-143, 325, 332`). The deferred claim of leaf в„“ is `v_в„“ = monster_eval(c_в„“)` above. Given K same-shape claims `{(c_в„“, v_в„“)}`, prove all K with one argument whose dominant verifier cost is a fixed FRI/opening endgame вҖ” independent of N and K вҖ” above a sub-dominant KВ·polylog eq/h_op residual.

### 3.1 Algebraic decomposition

Define three per-row **virtual** columns for claim в„“ (never committed):
`E_x^в„“[t] = eq(x_t, r_x'^в„“)`, `E_y^в„“[t] = eq(y_t, r_y^в„“)` **[on current `922df33f` the value vector is segmented: `E_y[t] = R[y_t]`, the segmented tensor of the Addendum; the identity below re-derives to `b_в„“ = DМғ_seg([r_y, r_segment])` over the `(lw+1)`-var cube]**, and `E_g^в„“[t] = О»_and^{m_t+1} В· h_{op_t}^в„“ В· eq6(s_t, r_s^в„“)` вҖ” the last folds the whole "meta" weight (slot Г— shift-variant Г— amount) into an 11-bit index `u_t = (s_tвҖ–op_tвҖ–m_t)`. Then

```
v_в„“ = ОЈ_t E_x^в„“[t] В· E_y^в„“[t] В· E_g^в„“[t]
```

вҖ” a **product of three multilinears** over `t вҲҲ B_25`, i.e. a degree-3 sumcheck claim. This is `evaluate_matrices` + `evaluate_monster_multilinear_for_operation` re-associated, nothing more.

Two identities carry the reduction:

- **(D) Marginalization.** For the ПҒ-weighted histograms `D_x(a;ПҒ) = ОЈ_t eq(t,ПҒ)В·вҹҰx_t=aвҹ§` (and `D_y, D_g`), the reduced column evals *are* the histogram MLEs at the claim's own point: `бәј_x^в„“(ПҒ) = DМғ_x(r_x'^в„“)`, etc.
- **(G) Eight-point split of H.** In char 2, `О»^{m0} = (О»+1)В·eq(m0, (О»+1)^{-1}+1)` and `О»^{2m1} = (О»+1)ВІВ·eq(m1, ((О»+1)ВІ)^{-1}+1)` (needs `О»_and вҲү {0,1}`, FS-random, abort вүӨ 2/2^128 per claim); with `H^в„“(op) = ОЈ_{oвҲҲ[8]} h_o^в„“ В· eq3(op, bits(o))`, `E_g^в„“` reduces to **8** evaluations of `DМғ_g` at fixed shift-variant points. So each claim's three column-evals become `a_в„“ = DМғ_x(В·)`, `b_в„“ = DМғ_y(В·)` **[re-derived to `b_в„“ = DМғ_seg([r_y, r_segment])` on current main, one M_D claim over the segmented cube вҖ” see Addendum]**, and 8 values `d_{в„“,o} = DМғ_g(В·)` вҖ” all evaluations of a single histogram oracle `M_D` (blocks `D_x | D_seg | D_g`) at points determined by `c_в„“`.

### 3.2 The committed table (= the vkey)

Per shape (STEP 2), commit three scalar **address** columns over `t вҲҲ B_25` вҖ” `X[t] = О№(x_t)+Оә_x`, `Y[t] = О№(y_t)+Оә_y`, `U[t] = О№(u_t)+Оә_u` вҖ” laid out as one 27-var oracle `M_VK = [X|Y|U|0]` via `fri::commit_interleaved` (`crates/iop-prover/src/fri/commit.rs:39-91`, transcript-free). Its digest **is the verification key**. `О№` embeds address bit `k` to basis element `ОІ_k`, `k вҲҲ [0,23)`; `V := О№(B_23)`. The block tags are `Оә_c = О№'(c)` mapping into the **next two** basis elements `ОІ_23, ОІ_24` (Оә_x=0, Оә_y=ОІ_23, Оә_u=ОІ_24, Оә_pad=ОІ_23+ОІ_24). Nothing here is secret вҖ” the discharge uses **no ZK masking anywhere**, which sidesteps both the mask-reuse hazards and your `log_batch_size==1` batched-BaseFold restriction (see В§6).

### 3.3 The KвҶ’1 reduction (four phases, one transcript)

**Phase 0 вҖ” absorb + structural preconditions.** Observe the full VK metadata blob (`vk_digest, cs_digest, N, N_pad, parity, dims, FRIParams, hash-suite id, row-order version`) into the transcript **before** sampling any challenge. Then assert, natively, structural facts that are probability-free: `cs_digest == H(SerializeBytes(leaf_cs))` (canonical versioned serialization exists at `constraint_system.rs:607-616`); all-BitAnd admission (every `MulConstraint` is the empty-operand padding default, `constraint_system.rs:560-574`, so `intmul_part вүЎ 0` and `monster_eval` = the T-sum exactly); single-source and coverage (В§4).

**Phase A вҖ” one batched degree-3 sumcheck over `t`.** `sumcheck::batch_verify(n_vars=25, degree=3, sums=[v_в„“]_1^K, channel)` (`crates/ip/src/sumcheck/batch.rs:37-60`) вҶ’ shared point ПҒ. The prover is a vendored cubic-product sumcheck (a ~120-line mirror of your `BivariateProductSumcheckProver`, `crates/ip-prover/src/sumcheck/bivariate_product.rs:19-27`, driven by `batch_prove_and_write_evals`, `.../batch.rs:126-141`). At `finish` it writes the three multilinear evals per claim `(a_в„“, b_в„“, g_в„“)`; the verifier checks `Horner(Ој; a_в„“В·b_в„“В·g_в„“) == e_A` (your intmul `verify_phase_3` multi-point pattern, `crates/verifier/src/protocols/intmul/verify.rs:111-183`), then receives the 8K values `d_{в„“,o}` and checks `g_в„“ == ОЈ_o Оі_{в„“,o}В·d_{в„“,o}` (Оі from `evaluate_h_op`, transparent). By (D)/(G) this is now `10K` MLE-eval claims on `M_D`.

**Phase B вҖ” 10K(+1) claims вҶ’ one point Пғ.** Your `BivariateProductSumcheckProver::new([W_eq, M_D], ОЈПҶ^i e_i)` with `W_eq = ОЈ_i ПҶ^iВ·eq(В·, p_i)`; verifier `sumcheck::verify(25, 2, sum, channel)` (`crates/ip/src/sumcheck/verify.rs:39-63`) вҶ’ Пғ, then `mВ·WМғ_eq(Пғ) == e_B` with `WМғ_eq(Пғ) = ОЈ_i ПҶ^iВ·eq_ind(p_i, Пғ)` computed via your `eq_ind` (`eq.rs:198-203`) вҖ” this is the only KВ·O(|point|) work in the verifier (the sub-dominant residual noted throughout).

**Phase C (STEP 2) вҖ” well-formedness of `M_D` vs `M_VK` via one weighted fracaddcheck.** The prover commits `M_D`; the verifier samples П„ and runs `fracaddcheck::verify(k=27, {num_eval:0, den_eval:d_root, point:[]}, channel)` (`crates/ip/src/fracaddcheck.rs:26-84`) on the claimed rational identity: `ОЈ_t eq(t,ПҒ)/(П„+X[t]) + вҖҰ/(П„+Y[t]) + вҖҰ/(П„+U[t]) + ОЈ_{a,c} M_D(a,c)/(П„+emb(a,c)) вүЎ 0`. With the block tags **coset-disjoint** (В§3.5), partial-fraction uniqueness forces, pole by pole, `D_x(a) = ОЈ_{t: X[t]=О№(a)+Оә_x} eq(t,ПҒ)` вҖ” the histogram definition. This is a **weighted** (coefficient-matching) use of fractional sums: numerators are field eq-weights, not integer multiplicities, so the char-2 even-multiplicity cancellation attack on LogUp-style counting does not apply. (This is our one "please sanity-check against the literature" item, В§7.) The prover layer split is on the top variable (`crates/ip-prover/src/fracaddcheck.rs:69-70`, via `split_half_ref`), so a blocks-high layout is prover-natural.

**Final check.** STEP 1: the verifier natively rebuilds `M_D` from the CS (whose digest it checked) in one O(N) pass and asserts `MМғ_D(Пғ) == m` вҖ” this collapses `K Г— (51.7M + rebuild)` to **one** table pass. STEP 2: two `basefold::verify` openings вҖ” `M_D` at Пғ, and `M_VK` via a corner trick (the prover already sent the three block corner values; sample `ПҒ_c вҲҲ FВІ` and open the combined `MМғ_VK([Пғ, ПҒ_c])` against `vk_digest`).

### 3.4 Soundness вҖ” S1 single-source (the headline)

The object the discharge certifies is not "a value `v_в„“`" in the abstract вҖ” it is **the specific wire leaf в„“'s `check_eval` multiplies.** `check_eval` asserts `witness_eval В· monster_eval == eval` (`shift/verify.rs:315-316`). In your native protocol the *other two* factors are already fixed: `witness_eval` is `recv_one`'d at `shift/verify.rs:174` and then PCS-bound вҖ” it is fed as the evaluation claim into `ring_switch::verify(shift_output.witness_eval(), вҖҰ)` (`verify.rs:234`), whose reduced claim becomes the `claim` of `verify_oracle_relations` on the committed `trace_oracle` (`verify.rs:256-260`; `trace_oracle = recv_oracle()` at `:130`); and `eval` is the shift-sumcheck output (`shift/verify.rs:167-170`), determined by that reduction. (Your own note at `shift/verify.rs:310-314` is a completeness remark вҖ” you *could* compute `witness_eval = eval/monster_eval` but that needs inverting a random element, so you read it instead; it is not a statement that `witness_eval` is unbound.) So natively the equation has force *precisely because `monster_eval` is also fixed* вҖ” computed in-line as the true public value вҖ” which is what pins the committed witness to the reduced claim.

Under deferral this last anchor is removed: the closure at `:254` is skipped and `monster_eval` becomes a free inout wire, while `:316` still asserts in-circuit. With a **free** third factor, `witness_eval В· monster_eval == eval` is satisfiable for *any* committed `witness_eval` and *any* reduced `eval` вҖ” a cheating prover just sets the wire to `eval В· witness_eval^{-1}`. The equation imposes nothing; the bindings of `witness_eval` and `eval` go vacuous. The **only** thing that restores force is pinning this wire to its true value `monster_true(c)`. That is what the discharge is for вҖ” and it must pin the **same** wire `:315` multiplies. If the discharge instead certifies a *decoupled* second value (e.g. a second read of `v_в„“`), the multiplied wire stays free and `:316` stays vacuous: a clean forgery, independent of the discharge's own soundness. In our wrap this is structural вҖ” the value is materialized exactly once, at the `compute_public_value` site (`:254`), and consumed only at `:315-316`; Phase A's target is that same materialized wire.

### 3.5 Soundness вҖ” Fiat-Shamir binding of the table, and char-2 subtleties

- **The commitment must be observed before the challenge.** `basefold::verify` takes `codeword_commitment` as an **argument** and never absorbs it (`crates/iop/src/basefold.rs:57-106`), and FRI query indices are `transcript.sample_bits(index_bits)` (`crates/iop/src/fri/verify.rs:200`). So a `vk_digest` (or `digest_D`) that is not observed into the transcript is **not bound by the queries**, and admits a table-swap: a prover picks the table after seeing ПҒ. Phase 0 observation is a hard soundness step, not hygiene. (In your own non-recursive flow the trace commitment *is* absorbed, via `recv_oracle` at `verify.rs:130` вҖ” so this is a requirement on *our* discharge, not a defect in yours.)
- **Coset-disjoint tags.** For Phase C to isolate poles per block, the tag *differences* must lie outside `V`. "Field-distinct" tags are insufficient вҖ” cosets of the same F2-subspace collide unless `Оә_c вҠ• Оә_c' вҲү V`. Constructing `Оә_c = О№'(c)` over `ОІ_23, ОІ_24` gives `Оә_c вҠ• Оә_c' вҲҲ span(ОІ_23,ОІ_24)\{0}`, disjoint from `V` by construction (asserted at VKGen). Bonus: `emb(a,c) = О№(a)+О№'(c)` is then plainly linear, so the Phase-C den column is transparent.
- **Parity.** We pad T to `N_pad` with copies of one fixed dummy tuple. In char 2 an *even* number of copies cancels identically in every sum; since N has no parity guarantee, we don't require evenness вҖ” instead `parity := (N_padвҲ’N) mod 2` is frozen into the VK, and Phase A runs on `v_в„“ + parityВ·w_d(c_в„“)`, where `w_d` is the single unpaired dummy row's weight (~60 transparent mults, reusing `eq(0,r)=1+r`, `eq.rs:212-215`). Exact, and it preserves S1 (the correction is public-derived arithmetic on the one recv'd `v_в„“`).

**Reduction chain.** S1 + coverage (structural) вҮ’ the certified element *is* each leaf's `check_eval` multiplicand, for every leaf; PCS binding вҮ’ `M_D` fixed; Phase C вҮ’ `M_D` = ПҒ-histograms of the committed columns; Phase B вҮ’ the 10K+1 eval claims true; (D),(G)+parity (exact algebra) вҮ’ `a_в„“,b_в„“,d_{в„“,o}` are true marginals; Phase A вҮ’ every `v_в„“ = monster_eval(c_в„“)` whp. Error budget (|F|=2^128, 96-bit target): Phase A `(KвҲ’1)/2^128 + 75/2^128`; Phase B `10K/2^128 + 50/2^128`; Phase C rational-identity SZ `вүӨ 2^-100`; total `вүӘ 2^-96` for `K вүӨ 2^20`. The one trust root (STEP 2 only): that the committed columns are the canonical table of the digest's CS вҖ” discharged by deterministic regeneration audit, the same trust class as the CS itself. STEP 1 has no such root (it rebuilds `M_D` from the CS directly).

### 3.6 Scope

This is **one flat aggregation level**: K same-shape leaves вҶ’ one combined proof. We deliberately do *not* claim to discharge the outer IronSpartan wiring evals вҖ” those use a different seam (`spartan-verifier/src/lib.rs:197-218` public closure via `compute_public_value`, the call at `:207`; `:226-253` `verify_oracle_relations` + `TransparentEvalFn`) and a different kernel (`eqВ·eqВ·О»^m`, `wiring.rs:41-99`). A strict-simplification "Variant B" table discharges those too (one derived point per claim, no 8Г— blowup), but it is unbuilt and out of scope here. The general (non-AND-only) leaf needs a second lane/table for the `MulConstraint` slots `(a,b,lo,hi) вҶҰ О»_int^{1..4}` (`shift/verify.rs:289-306`); mechanical, same shape.

## 4. Integration with the ZK-wrap

The discharge runs as one more inner segment of the flat multi-inner wrap, **after** the K leaf IOP verifications, on the same transcript. Because Phases A/B/C use only `IPVerifierChannel` operations (`batch_verify`, `sumcheck::verify`, `fracaddcheck::verify`, recv/sample/assert), they symbolically execute through `IronSpartanBuilderChannel` into outer constraints exactly like everything else вҖ” measured in-circuit cost вүҲ 150вҖ“250K B128 mults at K=64 (grows with K, the residual term), versus KГ—51.7M removed. Only the PCS openings (STEP 2) / the native pass (STEP 1) escape the channel abstraction (Merkle queries do; precedent `crates/iop/src/basefold_zk_channel.rs`) and join the final verifier's native endgame alongside the wrap's own combined opening.

**The seam.** We interpose a substituting channel during each leaf's verify. **On current `922df33f` there are TWO `compute_public_value` sites in `check_eval`** вҖ” `MonsterEvalFn` (`shift/verify.rs:316`, the O(N) deferred monster) and `PublicWordsEvalFn` (`:328`, an O(public) MLE of the verifier's own public words; see Addendum). The substituting/recorder channel records **both** and discharges only the monster (selected by arity вҖ” the public site is O(public), already in budget, and the guest evaluates it natively). At the monster site it (1) asserts `|inputs|` equals the shape arity вҖ” **our** check вҖ” and relies on your public-tag enforcement: `compute_public_value` forwards to `combine_varlen`, whose non-public branch hard-`panic!`s (`builder_channel.rs:234-238`), so a `recv`-derived input can never silently slip through (the trait doc at `channel.rs:99` calls this a "debug-assert," but the `IronSpartanBuilderChannel` impl is an unconditional panic); (2) supplies the prover-claimed monster value as the single materialized wire вҖ” **dropping the closure un-invoked**, exactly as your symbolic builder does (`channel.rs:101-103`); (3) records `(c_в„“, v_в„“)` to a sink; (4) returns that wire, which `:315-316` then multiplies. Coverage assert: after K leaves, the sink holds exactly K claims and Phase A's targets are exactly the sink's values (parity-corrected). An undischarged `v_в„“` leaves that leaf's `check_eval` vacuous (В§3.4), so coverage is a soundness step.

> **Correction to our own spec worth flagging:** an earlier draft made the seam a second `recv_one` of `v_в„“`. That is unsound *and* unimplementable in your wrap вҖ” unsound because a second read is exactly the decoupled wire of В§3.4 (the multiplied wire stays free), and unimplementable because wrap recvs are OTP-encrypted (`builder_channel.rs:175-182`: `recv_one` returns `inout вҲ’ key`, the precommit-segment pad вҖ” the native verifier never learns the plaintext). The correct and only sound seam is `compute_public_value` substitution, with that materialization as the single S1 binding site. This is what we built and audited.

## 6. Findings in binius64, and what we had to vendor

Neutral peer feedback, precise:

**(i) x86 GHASH is compile-time cfg-gated with no runtime dispatch вҶ’ silent software fallback.** `crates/field/src/arch/x86_64/packed_ghash_{128,256,512}.rs` gate on `#[cfg(target_feature = "pclmulqdq")]` (128, e.g. `:22,25,54,81,106`), `"vpclmulqdq"` (256, `:25,28,63,вҖҰ`), and `"vpclmulqdq"+"avx512f"` (512, e.g. `:23,26,61`). There is **no `is_x86_feature_detected!` anywhere in `crates/field`** вҖ” we grepped. Default `rustc` for `x86_64-unknown-linux-gnu` targets the 2003 baseline, which predates pclmul, so **every default-flags x86 build runs the field multiply in software.** Measured on an Ice Lake r6i.8xlarge (real proof): native verify **9.218 s вҶ’ 0.784 s (11.8Г—)** with `-C target-cpu=native`, constant across thread counts (804 `(v)pclmul` + 60 GFNI in the native disassembly); prove **5.1вҖ“5.5Г—**. Apple Silicon is unaffected (aarch64 enables clmul by default), which is why the penalty is invisible on dev machines. Because selection is compile-time with no fallback, a binary built with a feature the host lacks **SIGILLs** вҖ” so a portable binary is stuck in software today.
